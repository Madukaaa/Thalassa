The European Eel

The European Eel, a migratory fish species found in rivers across Europe, is critically endangered due to factors including habitat loss, overfishing, and climate change. Known for its unique life cycle involving migration to the Sargasso Sea for spawning, populations have declined drastically in recent decades. Conservation efforts include restrictions on fishing, habitat restoration, and international cooperation to protect remaining eel populations and facilitate their migration routes.
